## Idea 1: "Maritime Industrial Heritage"

**Design Movement:** Industrial Brutalism meets Maritime Vernacular — inspired by the raw, honest materials of Nova Scotia's working landscapes: weathered wood, exposed concrete, steel beams, and the rugged Atlantic coastline.

**Core Principles:**
1. Raw Authenticity — nothing polished to the point of feeling corporate; textures and materials should feel real
2. Structural Honesty — the layout itself should feel "built," with visible grid lines and construction-inspired dividers
3. Maritime Warmth — despite the industrial edge, warm tones from Nova Scotia's autumn palette soften the experience
4. Functional Clarity — information is organized like a blueprint: clear, precise, no wasted space

**Color Philosophy:**
- Primary: Deep charcoal (#2C2C2C) — like fresh asphalt or steel
- Accent: Safety orange (#D4622B) — construction signage, high-visibility vests
- Warm neutral: Sandstone (#E8DCC8) — Nova Scotia limestone
- Background: Off-white with subtle grain (#F5F2ED) — like concrete forms
- Text: Near-black (#1A1A1A) on light, cream (#F0EBE3) on dark

**Layout Paradigm:** Blueprint Grid — content organized in asymmetric columns with visible construction-line dividers. Sections overlap slightly like layered building plans. Full-bleed images break the grid intentionally.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Diagonal cut sections (like steel beam angles) between major content blocks
2. A subtle dot-grid pattern overlay on backgrounds, reminiscent of graph paper
3. Thick horizontal rules with small "measurement tick" marks

**Interaction Philosophy:** Solid and deliberate — elements don't bounce or float. They slide into place with weight, like heavy materials being set. Hover states reveal depth (shadow increases) rather than colour changes.

**Animation:** Sections enter with a slow upward translate (like a crane lifting), opacity fading in. Parallax on hero image. Counter animations for stats (years of experience, projects completed).

**Typography System:**
- Display: "DM Serif Display" — authoritative, grounded serif for headings
- Body: "Work Sans" — clean, industrial sans-serif
- Accent: Monospace snippets for phone numbers and addresses (like blueprint annotations)

## Idea 2: "Nova Scotia Craft Modern"

**Design Movement:** Scandinavian Functionalism adapted to Atlantic Canada — clean lines, natural materials, and a deep respect for craftsmanship. Inspired by the intersection of modern architecture and Nova Scotia's natural beauty.

**Core Principles:**
1. Craft Over Flash — let the quality of the work speak; design supports content, never overshadows it
2. Natural Palette — colours drawn directly from the Antigonish landscape: forest, stone, ocean, sky
3. Generous Breathing Room — sections are spacious, giving each piece of content room to land
4. Quiet Confidence — the design whispers expertise rather than shouting it

**Color Philosophy:**
- Primary: Forest green (#2D4A3E) — Nova Scotia spruce forests
- Accent: Copper (#B87333) — aged hardware, warm metallic
- Light: Birch white (#FAF8F5) — clean, natural warmth
- Dark: Slate (#3A3D42) — Cape Breton stone
- Muted: Fog grey (#C4C8CC) — morning mist over Antigonish harbour

**Layout Paradigm:** Stacked editorial — large hero with overlapping card, then alternating left-right content blocks with generous vertical rhythm. No sidebar. Content flows like a well-crafted magazine spread.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin copper accent lines that appear as section separators and hover underlines
2. Rounded-corner photo frames with a subtle inner shadow, like looking through a window
3. A small house/hammer icon mark used as a recurring motif

**Interaction Philosophy:** Gentle and refined — smooth transitions, subtle scale on hover, soft focus effects on images. Nothing aggressive. The experience should feel like walking through a well-built home.

**Animation:** Fade-up on scroll with staggered timing for grouped elements. Smooth parallax on the hero. Service cards lift slightly on hover with a soft shadow bloom.

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Playfair Display" — elegant serif with character
- Body: "Source Sans 3" — highly readable, professional
- Accent: Small caps for labels and categories

## Idea 3: "Bold Builder"

**Design Movement:** Contemporary Construction Branding — inspired by modern construction company identities that lean into bold geometry, high contrast, and unapologetic scale. Think large-format typography, dramatic photography, and confident colour blocking.

**Core Principles:**
1. Scale and Impact — oversized typography and full-bleed imagery create immediate visual authority
2. High Contrast — dark backgrounds with bright accents demand attention
3. Geometric Structure — sharp angles, clean blocks, no soft curves
4. Action-Oriented — every section drives toward a call to action

**Color Philosophy:**
- Primary: Deep navy (#0F1923) — authority, trust, nighttime construction site
- Accent: Vivid amber (#F5A623) — warning lights, heavy equipment, energy
- White: Pure (#FFFFFF) — for text on dark, creating maximum contrast
- Grey: Steel (#6B7280) — secondary text, subtle backgrounds
- Highlight: Electric blue (#2563EB) — links and interactive elements

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-width cinematic blocks — each section is a full-viewport scene. Hero takes 100vh. Services displayed in a dramatic grid with large numbers. Testimonials on a dark band. Contact as a bold split-screen.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Large stencil-style numbers (01, 02, 03) labelling each section
2. Angled clip-path dividers between sections creating a dynamic, forward-leaning feel
3. A thick yellow accent bar that appears as an underline motif throughout

**Interaction Philosophy:** Confident and snappy — quick transitions, bold hover states (background colour fills), elements that feel decisive. No hesitation in the UI.

**Animation:** Clip-path reveals on scroll. Text slides in from the left with a slight overshoot. Numbers count up rapidly. Hero has a slow Ken Burns zoom on the background image.

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Bebas Neue" — tall, condensed, commanding
- Body: "Inter" — clean and modern for readability
- Accent: Uppercase tracking-wide for labels and CTAs
